Why Your Google Business Profile Matters

When someone's furnace dies at 10pm in January, they're not browsing websites. They're Googling "emergency HVAC near me" and calling whoever shows up first.

Your Google Business Profile (GBP) determines:

  • Whether you show up in the "Local Pack" (the map results)
  • How trustworthy you look compared to competitors
  • Whether customers call you or scroll past

The problem? Most HVAC companies set up their profile once and never touch it again. Meanwhile, they're bleeding leads to competitors who've optimized theirs.

1

Wrong or Missing Service Area

Your GBP has a "Service Area" setting that tells Google where you actually work. If this is wrong, you're invisible to customers in your best territories.

The problem: Many HVAC companies only list their shop address. Google then assumes you only serve that immediate area. Customers 15 miles away? They'll never see you.

Even worse: Some profiles have service areas from when the business started ‚Äî maybe just one town ‚Äî but the company now serves the whole county.

‚úÖ The Fix (5 minutes):
  1. Go to your Google Business Profile
  2. Click "Edit profile" ‚Üí "Location"
  3. Under "Service area," add ALL cities/zip codes you serve
  4. You can add up to 20 areas ‚Äî use them all if relevant
  5. Be honest ‚Äî don't add areas you won't actually service quickly
Pro tip: If you serve a wide area, add both city names AND zip codes. Some people search "HVAC 75001" while others search "HVAC Plano TX."
2

Your business description is prime real estate for keywords ‚Äî and most HVAC companies waste it with generic fluff or leave it empty.

‚úÖ Good Example

Why this matters: Google reads your description to understand what you do. "Quality service and customer satisfaction" tells Google nothing. "AC repair, furnace installation, 24/7 emergency" tells Google exactly what searches to show you for.

‚úÖ The Fix (10 minutes):
  1. List your main services: AC repair, furnace installation, duct cleaning, etc.
  2. Include your service areas by name
  3. Mention key differentiators: 24/7 service, same-day, licensed, insured
  4. Keep it natural ‚Äî don't stuff keywords awkwardly
  5. Use all 750 characters if you can
3

No Posts or Updates (Dead Profile)

Google rewards active businesses. A profile with no posts in 6 months looks abandoned ‚Äî because to Google, maybe it is.

The reality: Google Business Profile has a built-in posting feature that most HVAC companies completely ignore. You can post updates, offers, and events ‚Äî and they show up right on your profile.

Why it matters:

  • Active profiles rank higher in local search
  • Posts give customers more reasons to choose you
  • Seasonal offers (AC tune-up specials) drive immediate calls
  • It signals to Google that you're a real, active business
‚úÖ The Fix (15 minutes/week):
  1. Post at least once per week ‚Äî even a simple update helps
  2. Share seasonal promotions (spring AC tune-up, fall furnace check)
  3. Post before/after photos of jobs (with customer permission)
  4. Announce new services or certifications
  5. Share tips: "3 signs your AC needs service before summer"
Easy content ideas:
  • Monday: Quick maintenance tip
  • Wednesday: Job photo or customer shoutout
  • Friday: Weekend emergency service reminder
4

Missing or Wrong Business Hours

If your hours are wrong ‚Äî or missing ‚Äî customers assume you're closed. And they'll call your competitor instead.

Common problems:

  • Hours show 9-5 but you actually answer calls until 7pm
  • No "special hours" for holidays (customers assume you're closed)
  • No indication of 24/7 emergency service
  • Hours haven't been updated since COVID
‚úÖ The Fix (5 minutes):
  1. Update your regular hours to match when you ACTUALLY answer the phone
  2. Add "More hours" for specific services (e.g., "Emergency service: 24 hours")
  3. Set special hours for holidays in advance
  4. If you offer 24/7 emergency service, make sure it shows!
Warning: If a customer calls during your listed hours and nobody answers, that damages trust immediately. Only list hours you can actually cover.
5

No Photos (Or Only a Logo)

Profiles with photos get 42% more requests for directions and 35% more website clicks. Yet most HVAC profiles have zero photos or just a logo.

What customers want to see:

  • Your team (real people = trust)
  • Your trucks (professional fleet = established business)
  • Completed jobs (proof you do good work)
  • Your shop/office (you're a real local business)

What they don't want: Stock photos, just a logo, or nothing at all.

‚úÖ The Fix (20 minutes once, then ongoing):
  1. Add a professional cover photo (your team or branded truck)
  2. Upload photos of your team in uniform
  3. Add 5-10 photos of completed jobs
  4. Include a photo of your shop/office exterior
  5. Make it a habit: snap a quick photo after each job and upload weekly
Photo tips:
  • Natural lighting beats flash
  • Clean, organized shots (no cluttered backgrounds)
  • Show your technicians smiling ‚Äî people hire people they like
  • Before/after shots are gold

Quick-Fix Checklist

Print this and check off as you go:

  • Service area includes all cities/zips you actually serve
  • Business description has specific services + locations
  • Posted at least one update in the last week
  • Business hours match when you actually answer
  • Emergency/after-hours service is clearly indicated
  • At least 10 photos uploaded (team, trucks, jobs)
  • Cover photo is professional (not just a logo)
  • All reviews have responses (good and bad)
  • Phone number is correct and tested
  • Website link goes to the right page
